Joseph Stalin

Joseph Stalin: Architect of Stalinism and Leader of the Soviet Union

Joseph Stalin (1883-1953) was a Soviet Union leader during the Second World War.

Timeline of Joseph Stalin

  • 1878 - Born in Gori, Georgia.
  • 1912 - Becomes General Secretary of the Communist Party, consolidating power.
  • 1928 - Launches ambitious Five-Year Plans to industrialize the Soviet Union.
  • 1932 - Implements agricultural collectivization, leading to the Holodomor and mass famine.
  • 1936-1938 - Oversees the Great Purge, executing and imprisoning millions of perceived enemies.
  • 1941-1945 - Leads the USSR during World War II, contributing to the defeat of Nazi Germany.
  • 1949 - Establishes the Soviet Union as a nuclear power.
  • 1953 - Dies in Moscow, leaving behind a complex legacy of modernization and terror.

“He had found Russia working with wooden ploughs and leaving it equipped with atomic piles.”
